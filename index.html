Small’s didn’t start in a boardroom.

It started in a backyard.

After years as a professional snowboarder, Jordan Small found himself back home when the world shut down. What began as casual backyard grilling and smoking meats quickly turned into an obsession with smash burgers — pressing patties hard on the flat-top to get those perfect crispy edges.

Before the first brick-and-mortar ever opened, Small’s was popping up all over SoCal — at breweries, parking lots, events, and anywhere with a griddle and a hungry crowd. Hundreds of pop-ups later, the same goal remains: crispier edges, better bite, no shortcuts, no fillers.

This isn’t a concept. It’s reps.

This is Small’s.

Flat-top

The build

  1. Press

    Cold beef meets screaming steel. Maximum weight. If you don’t see the lace, we didn’t do it right.

  2. Stack

    Develop the crust, then melt the cheese. Sharp cheese finds every crevice. The edges stay loud; the middle stays juicy.

  3. Send

    House sauce, pickles for acid, straight off the iron and into your hand. Eat it while it’s still shouting.
